About

Fifty years ago, Madison, Wisconsin was a cradle of what came to be known as “the counterculture,” a rich mix of music, art, politics, history and participatory experience. The Madison Reunion is a destination for all those interested in the place and the period—three days of lectures, performances, displays, meetings, meals and discussions, a kind of cultural Woodstock, to examine where we are, how we got here and what we left behind.

Where & When

From June 14 through June 16, 2018, The Madison Reunion will be held throughout the campus and State Street, free and open to all. We will sponsor bulletin boards (to help you find your old friends) tours (that place you used to live; what is it now?) films (when was the last time you saw Easy Rider or Zabriskie Point?) displays and exhibits.

The Conference on Madison will be held during the same three days at the Memorial Union, with attendance by pre-registration only. The Conference is limited to 1,000 attendees; a single registration provides access to all panels.
